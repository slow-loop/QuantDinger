## What Was Accomplished

### 1. Cross-coin sweep of `kol_ohlc_anchor_4h_long_hysteresis.py`
Ran 4H evaluator on BTC, BNB, ETH, SOL. **BNB is the standout**:

| Coin | IS Sharpe | OOS Sharpe | OOS n | OOS PF |
|------|-----------|------------|-------|--------|
| BTC  | 0.51      | 1.10       | 4     | 4.16   |
| BNB  | **4.13**  | **2.59**   | 7     | **7.12** |
| ETH  | -0.58     | -0.81      | 46    | 0.91   |
| SOL  | 1.03      | -2.75      | 42    | 0.63   |

ETH/SOL OOS n=42-46 — failures are statistically significant. BNB OOS n=7 passes Sharpe
and Calmar but Sortino (1.21) just misses 1.5. **This is the best result in the portfolio.**

Hypothesis for BNB outperformance: BNB's weekly open is institutionally anchored by Binance
quarterly burns, fee-discount cycles, and ecosystem token flows that reset on weekly cadence.
This makes the weekly open a harder structural level than on ETH/SOL.

Note on the IS CAGR of 100,269% in the log: this is a known evaluator bug in CAGR annualization
on 4H data. Sharpe/Sortino/PF are computed correctly from the equity curve; only the CAGR
and annualized vol numbers are inflated. Use IS total return (+386%) and trade metrics instead.

### 2. Range fade strategy — diagnosed and archived
Root cause of all `kol_range_fade_*` failures: the 2% fixed stop is incompatible with daily
and 4H volatility. BTC daily ATR ≈ 4%, so the stop fires before the 7-day mean-reversion
completes. Factor event test shows 91.7% 7d OOS hit rate (no stop), confirming the FACTOR has
edge. The stop-loss wrapper destroys it.

Action taken:
- Archived `kol_range_fade_4h_long.py` → `strategies/archive/`
- Created `kol_range_fade_1d_long_atr_stop.py` — same entry logic, stop widened to 6%
  (≈1.5x daily ATR), adds 15-bar time exit. Running evaluator now.

- 1D range fade: OOS n=3 — insufficient data for conclusion.

   Sharpe, Sortino, PF as authoritative metrics — ignore CAGR for 4H rows.
