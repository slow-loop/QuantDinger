### Root Cause Analysis

Despite the regime-matching logic (mean-reversion is theoretically suited to 2025-26
choppy regime), the implementation exposed a structural flaw in how Z-score mean-reversion
works on crypto fat-tailed distributions:

1. **Entry too early**: Z-score = 2.0 threshold is triggered by normal noise in crypto.
   The actual mean-reversion setup requires Z-score to *peak and turn* (dZ/dt sign change)
   not just cross 2.0. Crossing 2.0 frequently precedes extension to Z=4-5.

2. **Negative R:R trap**: ATR 2x stop is hit frequently on extension legs (~60-70% of stops
   fire). When price eventually reverts, the strategy often exits via timeout, missing the
   full recovery. Result: many small losses + a few wins that don't compensate.

3. **Regime-misread**: "Mean-reversion should work in 2025-26" was valid logic but the
   specific mechanism (threshold-cross Z-score) does not map cleanly. Crypto mean-reversion
   more reliably fires after *multi-day extremes with volume exhaustion*, not on a single
   4H bar crossing 2σ.

4. **IS data leakage note**: IS Sharpe was +1.38 (BTC), appearing deceptively healthy.
   The 2020-2025 bull cycle has many extended low-Z-score periods where the strategy
   simply avoids the market, inflating IS metrics via inaction. OOS regime has shorter
   cycles and less clean Z-score distribution.

### Root Cause Analysis

Session 3 predicted that RSI divergence would avoid the "threshold-cross extension" problem
of Z-score. That prediction was incorrect in a specific way: RSI divergence *does* avoid
the false threshold-cross problem, but it introduces a different failure mode in a
persistent downtrend regime:

1. **Catch falling knife in downtrend**: When BTC/ETH is in a multi-month downtrend (2025-26),
   bullish RSI divergence fires repeatedly as each new lower low has less RSI momentum. But
   the divergence does NOT signal a reversal — it signals a weakening downtrend that may

2. **Payoff inversion**: OOS BTC win rate was 50% (coin flip) but payoff ratio was 0.25 —

   the strategy catches a brief relief rally (small), but losses occur when the trend extends

3. **ETH even worse**: Win rate 11% (1 of 9 trades) — near-total failure. ETH's downtrend in
   2025-26 was steeper than BTC's. Every divergence signal was a trap.

4. **IS deceptive again**: IS Sharpe +3.17 on BTC is very strong, driven by 2020-2024 bull
   cycles where bullish divergence after local pullbacks reliably worked. The same structural
   failure mode (downtrend extension) manifests in OOS only.
